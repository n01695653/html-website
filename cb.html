The ingridents used to make Chole Bature .

The Instructuion To Follow To Make Recipe .

  1. Cook the Chickpeas:
  2. Drain the soaked chickpeas and pressure cook them with water and salt until soft (about 3-4 whistles).
  3. Make the Masala:
  4. In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  5. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  6. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  7. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  8. Add Spices:
  9. Mix in turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Cook for a couple of minutes.
  10. Add the cooked chickpeas along with some water (adjust to desired consistency).
  11. Let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes. Stir in garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander.
  12. Prepare the Bhature
  13. Make the Dough:
  14. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
  15. Add yogurt and enough water to knead into a soft dough.
  16. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes
  17. Shape the Bhature:
  18. After resting, divide the dough into small balls.
    Roll each ball into a circle (about 6-7 inches in diameter) using a rolling pin. Ensure they are slightly thicker than regular chapatis.
  19. Fry the Bhature:
  20. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  21. Once hot, gently slide in one rolled bhature.
  22. Fry until it puffs up and turns golden brown on both sides.
  23. Remove and drain on paper towels. Repeat for the remaining dough.
  24. Serve
  25. Serve hot Chole with fluffy Bhature, along with some sliced onions, pickles, and yogurt on the side.
